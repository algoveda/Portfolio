Let's Talk

A creative journal about building this portfolio

1

What was your inspiration?

I was clear from the beginning about what I wanted to create. The styles I used were either inspired by existing ideas or something I had thought of long before. My main inspiration was to make something visually appealing, unique, and eye-catching.

#color-theory #design
2

How did you make it?

At first, I thought I’d learn all the coding languages and then start building. But I soon realized that even with that knowledge, I’d still use AI to enhance the design. So I just started. I used my vision as the base and let AI tools assist me in shaping it. Tools like Le Chat, Claude AI, and DeepSite helped me throughout the process.

Inspiration
#layout #inspiration

Key Challenge

Many pages needed AI assistance to build and explain them, but not all features worked as planned. I had to rethink and redesign quite a few parts. It became a constant process of tweaking the styles and making sure the pages didn’t look obviously AI-generated.

3

How did you fall in love with it?

As I kept building, I realized how much I enjoyed the whole process. It was fun, creative, and satisfying. I genuinely fell in love with this kind of work, and by the end of it, I knew this is something I’d love to do in the future.

1

LEARN TYPING

seriously, if you don’t know how to type, you’re never going to enjoy coding. You’ll always struggle, and that kills the fun. Most YouTubers ignore this part, but trust me, it’s something you need to focus on before even starting to learn coding. It’s a must-have skill.

#typing #learning
2

DIVE INTO HTML

This is honestly the easiest thing to learn—you can pick it up in just one day. Just sit with full focus, and you’ll be typing smoothly in no time. It’s a small effort that makes a huge difference when you start coding.

# development
#css #grid

Learning Tip

The best way to learn is by building real projects. Tutorials are great, but nothing beats the learning from solving actual problems you encounter while creating something.

3

CSS Breakthroughs

I styled my way into the web world with the help of SuperSimpleDev and Bro Code. One taught me the rules, the other showed me how to break them beautifully. From flexbox to fancy hover effects, I turned plain HTML into pixel-perfect art. With every line of CSS, I wasn't just coding—I was painting the web with style.

#javascript #learning
4

JavaScript Journey

I dove into the world of JavaScript with nothing but curiosity and the SuperSimpleDev YouTube channel as my guide. Each video felt like unlocking a new superpower—from making buttons click to bending the DOM to my will. No frameworks, no fluff—just pure vanilla JS magic. It was like learning to speak the true language of the web, one line at a time.

#javascript #learning

Essential Tools

VS Code is a must, it looks complicated at first, and many new devs get scared of it, but once you start using it, it's actually simple. Along with VS Code, you should also know different AI assistants and when to use which one for better results.

#tools #workflow

Helpful Resources

Whenever you get stuck, just ask AI assistants—they’re honestly a blessing for all of us. Don’t sit stuck for hours. And if you want to properly read and learn something, go for MDN Web Docs and W3Schools. People underrate them now, but they’re actually super helpful and clear.
